<p><p>The wine cork market predominantly features two types: natural corks and synthetic corks. Natural corks are derived from the bark of cork oak trees, offering a traditional and breathable seal that enhances aging potential. However, they can sometimes lead to cork taint. On the other hand, synthetic corks, made from materials like PVC or expanded polystyrene, provide a consistent and cost-effective alternative, ensuring no cork taint but lacking the same oxygen interaction, which can affect wine maturation.</p></p>
