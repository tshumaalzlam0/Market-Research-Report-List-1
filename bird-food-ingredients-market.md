<p><p>The bird food ingredients market is categorized into two main types: shelled and unshelled. Shelled ingredients, such as sunflower seeds and nuts, are processed to remove their outer shells, making them easier for birds to consume and reducing waste. Unshelled types, including whole seeds and grains, offer natural protection and longer shelf life but require more effort for birds to access the food. Each type caters to different bird species and consumer preferences, impacting market dynamics.</p></p>
