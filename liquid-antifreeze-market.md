<p><p>Liquid antifreeze is a chemical additive used primarily in automotive engines to lower the freezing point and raise the boiling point of the coolant, ensuring optimal engine performance in extreme temperatures. It typically contains ethylene glycol or propylene glycol as its base, along with corrosion inhibitors and other additives to protect engine components. Liquid antifreeze is essential for preventing engine damage caused by freezing, overheating, or corrosion.</p><p>The Liquid Antifreeze Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing vehicle production, rising demand for high-performance engines, and the need for efficient thermal management systems. Alcohols, like ethylene glycol and methanol, are widely used for their low freezing points and heat transfer properties. Alcohol ethers, such as propylene glycol ethers, offer enhanced stability and lower toxicity.
